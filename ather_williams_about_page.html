The background

A first-generation immigrant who made it to Harvard — and never forgot where he came from.

Ather Williams III is a first-generation immigrant from a blue-collar family. He got into Harvard on the strength of his SAT and AP scores — and found himself in a room where almost nobody looked like him or came from where he came from.

Rather than shrink, he found his superpower. Not being the smartest person in the room in any single specialty, but being the conductor of the orchestra — the one who could organise the problem-solving effort, see the whole board, and get people into the right positions to succeed.

That instinct — honed on Harvard's basketball courts as a point guard — has driven every role since. At JPMorgan. At Bank of America. At Wells Fargo. Almost always the only one in the room. Always comfortable in his own skin.

The philosophy

"Be the conductor of the orchestra — not first chair in any specialty."

Ather's leadership philosophy is rooted in four things: set a clear vision, align the resources, remove the roadblocks, and get out of the way of talented people. He leads from the front when it counts and distributes the ball when it doesn't need to be him.

He is passionately curious and constructively impatient. He doesn't suffer fools. And he believes that feedback — even uncomfortable feedback — is always a gift.

Above all, he believes in the power of diversity — of thought, experience, and perspective. Because people who look like him are too often misjudged. And he has spent thirty years proving them wrong.

What he values
01
Curiosity & a learning mentality
The only sustainable competitive advantage is an organisation's ability to learn faster than the competition. Ather is passionately curious — always on a quest for better.
02
Diversity of thought, experience & perspective
People misjudge him because of how he looks. That experience shaped a deep commitment to building teams and rooms where difference is the competitive advantage.
03
Empathy & psychological safety
You cannot build high-performing teams without trust. Ather creates climates where people feel safe to experiment, fail, learn, and bring their full selves to the work.
04
Honest communication
Communicate simply, openly, and respectfully — without diluting the message. Feedback is always a gift, even when it's not what you wanted to hear.
05
Self-reliance & accountability
There is no substitute for results. No excuses. The standard his mother set when she was diagnosed with MS — keep going, keep building, keep delivering — has never left him.
06
Mission & purpose
From fixing healthcare as a Board Director at Blue Shield of California to democratising access to global payments — every role must connect to something larger than the bottom line.
